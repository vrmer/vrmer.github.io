Who am I?

I am interested in Deep Learning used for multilingual Natural Language Processing, and especially in building better tools for typologically diverse and low resource languages. I am skilled in Python, and experienced in applying a number of complex models such as BERT, multilingual BERT, LaBSE, and LASER.

Currently, I am in my second year of my masters in Human Language Technology at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam organized by the CLTL lab, headed by Professor Piek Vossen. Prior to that, I completed a BA in Linguistics at the University of Cambridge.

Since September 2021, I am working for the company TAUS as an NLP intern. My project involves setting up experiments with an autoencoder, compressing high dimensional multilingual sentence embeddings (LASER, LaBSE, and USE) while retaining the original information they contain.
